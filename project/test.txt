\paragraph{Cartan basis.}  

Introduce the basis for the Lorentz and R symmetry generators
\begin{align}\label{cartan}
r \ \ &\doteq  - 2 (R^1_{~1} + R^2_{~2})  , \nn\\
R \ \ &\doteq  R^1_{~1} - R^2_{~2} , & 
j \ \ &\doteq  2\cM_1^{~1} =-2 \cM_2^{~2}, &
\tj \ \ &\doteq  -2\cMt^{\dot1}_{~\dot1} =2 \cMt^{\dot2}_{~\dot2}, \\
R^+ &\doteq  R^1_{~2} , & j^+ &\doteq  \cM_1^{~2} , & \tj^- &\doteq  \cMt^{\dot1}_{~\dot2} , \nn\\
R^- &\doteq  R^2_{~1} , & j^- &\doteq  \cM_2^{~1} , & \tj^+ &\doteq  \cMt^{\dot2}_{~\dot1} , \nn
\end{align}
These satisfy the $\su(2)$ algebra in the form $R^+\circ R^-=R$, $R\circ R^{\pm}=\pm 2 R^{\pm}$, and similarly for the $j$'s and $\tj$'s.  With this normalization the eigenvalues of $j$, $\tj$, and $R$ are integers.
$\u(1)_r$ charges of the supercharges are $r(Q)=-r(\Qt)=-r(S)=r(\St)=-1$.  
Label irreducible supermultiplets of operators as $[j;\tj]_D^{(R;r)}$ by their highest eigenvalues of the Cartan generators $D$, $j$, $\tj$, $R$, and $r$, which we denote by the same symbol as the corresponding generator.
These normalizations agree with the conventions of \red{[CDI]}.

There are three combinations of the five Cartan generators that will play a central role.
They are
\begin{align}\label{LLhZ}
L_0 &\doteq  \tfrac12 D + \tfrac14 j + \tfrac14 \tj, \nn\\
\hL_0 &\doteq  \tfrac12 D - \tfrac14 j - \tfrac14 \tj - \tfrac12 R, \nn\\
Z &\doteq  \ph{\tfrac12 D} - \tfrac14 j + \tfrac14 \tj + \tfrac14 r .
\end{align}
These agree with the definitions of the corresponding operators in \red{[VOA,Lemos]} except for the normalization of $Z$:  $Z=2\cZ$ where $\cZ$ is the central charge of \red{[VOA,Lemos]}.
We will generally use $\{L_0, \hL_0, Z, r, R\}$ as a basis of the Cartan generators, and denote their eigenvalues by $\{h, \hh, Z, r, R\}$.
The operators we are interested in will have $r, R, 2h \in \N$ and will generally have $\hL_0=Z=0$.
It is useful to note that
\begin{align}\label{}
D &= L_0 + \hL_0 + \tfrac12 R, &
j + \tj &= 2 L_0 - 2 \hL_0 - R, &
j - \tj &= r - 4 Z.\nn
\end{align}
Spinor and lightcone-complex basis diagonalize this Cartan.  Their eigenvalues are given below.

At the algebra level, all of these compositions are simple commutators.
How do we lift them to the action of the group element?


\section{Action of conf on supercharges}
The above bases choices give us the action of the algebra (of bnosonic generators) on the supercharges and superconformal charges.

Define vectors of charges as:
